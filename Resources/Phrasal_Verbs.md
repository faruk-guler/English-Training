# Most Common English Phrasal Verbs - En Sık Kullanılan Deyimsel Fiiller

Bu dosya, İngilizcede günlük konuşmada ve yazı dilinde en çok kullanılan deyimsel fiilleri (Phrasal Verbs), Türkçe anlamlarını ve örnek cümlelerini içermektedir.

| Phrasal Verb | Türkçe Anlamı | İngilizce Açıklaması | Örnek Cümle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bring up** | (Bir konuyu) açmak, gündeme getirmek; çocuk büyütmek | Mention a topic; raise a child | She brought up an interesting point during the meeting. / They brought up their children well. |
| **Call off** | İptal etmek | Cancel | The outdoor concert was called off because of the heavy rain. |
| **Carry on** | Devam etmek | Continue | Even though it was difficult, they carried on with their work. |
| **Carry out** | Uygulamak, yerine getirmek, gerçekleştirmek | Perform a task or research | The scientists are carrying out a series of experiments. |
| **Check out** | İncelemek, göz atmak; otelden çıkış yapmak | Investigate, look at; leave a hotel | You should check out that new restaurant downtown. / We need to check out before 11 AM. |
| **Come across** | Tesadüfen karşılaşmak / bulmak | Find or meet by chance | I came across my old high school teacher at the supermarket. |
| **Come up with** | (Fikir, çözüm vb.) bulmak, ortaya atmak | Produce an idea or plan | We need to come up with a new marketing strategy. |
| **Cut down on** | (Yiyecek, içecek, harcama vb.) kısmak, azaltmak | Reduce the amount of something | The doctor told him to cut down on sugar. |
| **End up** | Kendini beklenmedik bir durumda/yerde bulmak | Eventually reach a state or place | We took a wrong turn and ended up in a small village. |
| **Find out** | Öğrenmek, keşfetmek | Discover information | I need to find out what time the train leaves. |
| **Get along (with)** | Biriyle iyi geçinmek | Have a good relationship | Do you get along with your new colleagues? |
| **Get over** | Atlatmak, iyileşmek, üstesinden gelmek | Recover from an illness or disappointment | It took her a long time to get over the breakup. |
| **Give up** | Bırakmak, vazgeçmek | Stop trying; quit a habit | Never give up on your dreams. / He gave up smoking last year. |
| **Go on** | Devam etmek; meydana gelmek | Continue; happen | Please go on, I am listening to you. / What is going on here? |
| **Hold on** | Beklemek (genellikle kısa süre) | Wait for a short time | Hold on a minute, I'll check if she is available. |
| **Keep up (with)** | Hızına yetişmek, ayak uydurmak | Stay at the same level as | Technology changes so fast that it's hard to keep up with it. |
| **Look after** | Bakmak, ilgilenmek (çocuk, hasta, evcil hayvan vb.) | Take care of | Can you look after my cat while I am on vacation? |
| **Look forward to** | Dört gözle beklemek | Anticipate with pleasure | I am looking forward to meeting you next week. |
| **Look up** | (Sözlükte vb.) aramak; bilgi araştırmak | Search for information | If you don't know the word, look it up in the dictionary. |
| **Make up** | Uydurmak (hikaye, yalan vb.); barışmak; makyaj yapmak | Invent a story; reconcile; apply cosmetics | He made up an excuse for being late. / They argue a lot but always make up quickly. |
| **Pass out** | Bayılmak | Lose consciousness | It was so hot in the room that I almost passed out. |
| **Put off** | Ertelemek; soğutmak/tiksindirmek | Postpone; discourage | Never put off until tomorrow what you can do today. / The smell of the food put me off. |
| **Put up with** | Katlanmak, tahammül etmek | Tolerate | I cannot put up with this noise anymore! |
| **Run out of** | Tükenmek, bitmek (para, zaman, malzeme vb.) | Have no more of something left | We have run out of milk, I need to go to the store. |
| **Set up** | Kurmak (şirket, sistem vb.), düzenlemek | Establish, arrange | They set up a new business last month. / We need to set up a meeting. |
| **Show up** | Çıkagelmek, varmak, görünmek | Arrive, appear | He didn't show up for the interview yesterday. |
| **Take after** | (Bir aile büyüğüne) çekmek, benzemek | Resemble a parent or relative | She takes after her mother in both looks and character. |
| **Take off** | (Uçak için) havalanmak; (kıyafet vb.) çıkarmak; ani başarı kazanmak | Leave the ground; remove clothing; become successful | The plane took off on time. / Please take off your shoes. |
| **Turn down** | Reddetmek (teklif, başvuru vb.); sesini kısmak | Reject an offer; lower the volume | She turned down the job offer because the salary was too low. / Turn down the music. |
| **Work out** | Antrenman/spor yapmak; çözmek/yoluna koymak | Exercise; solve a problem | I work out at the gym three times a week. / I am sure things will work out in the end. |
